# AgriAlert — Presentation guide (non-technical audience)

Use the sections below as slide titles and bullet points. Shorten any block to fit your time slot.

---

## 1. Opening — What is AgriAlert?

- **AgriAlert** is a **Smart Farmer Weather Alert** web experience built for **Rwanda**.
- It helps **farmers and advisers** see **current weather** and a **five-day outlook** for any **district**, and **register** farm details so alerts can be matched to **district** and **crop**.
- It runs in a **web browser** on a phone or computer — no app store install required.

**One sentence for the title slide:**  
*“AgriAlert brings district weather and farm registration together in one simple website for Rwandan agriculture.”*

---

## 2. Why it matters (problem → opportunity)

- Weather affects **planting, irrigation, harvest**, and **risk** on the farm.
- Decisions are easier when **local conditions** and a **short forecast** are visible in one place.
- Recording **who farms where**, and **which crop** they focus on, supports **better targeting** of advisory messages.

*Keep this slide visual: photo of fields or farmers — you can use your `images/` folder assets.*

---

## 3. What users can do (features, plain language)

| User need | What AgriAlert offers |
|-----------|------------------------|
| “What is the weather in my district?” | **Home** page: search by **district name**, see **temperature**, **rain chance**, **humidity**, and a **five-day forecast**. |
| “Where is that on the map?” | A **small map** shows the **searched location** so users can **orient** themselves. |
| “How do I sign up for awareness?” | **Register** page: **name**, **phone or email**, **district**, **crop**, and **alert frequency** (instant or daily). |
| “What did I submit?” | **My alerts** lists **saved registrations** on that device so users can **review** or **clear** entries. |
| “Who is behind this?” | **About** explains the **purpose** and **steps** in simple terms. |
| “How do I get in touch?” | **Contact** opens the user’s **email** with a ready-to-send message to the **organisation address** shown on the page. |

---

## 4. The five main screens (for a “product tour” slide)

1. **Home** — District search, weather card, **five-day forecast**, **map**, call-to-action to register.  
2. **Register** — Form for **farm and contact** details.  
3. **My alerts** — **Table** of registrations the user has saved.  
4. **About** — **Mission** and **how to use** the site.  
5. **Contact** — **Enquiry form** that hands off to **email**.

---

## 5. User journey (story slide)

1. Open **AgriAlert** in the browser.  
2. On **Home**, pick or type a **Rwanda district** and tap **Check area**.  
3. Read **today’s conditions** and the **next five days**.  
4. See the **map** to confirm **where** the forecast applies.  
5. Go to **Register** to save **district**, **crop**, and **contact** preferences.  
6. Use **My alerts** anytime to see **what was registered**.

---

## 6. Trust and “real data” (say this without jargon)

- **Weather** comes from established **global forecast services** (updated regularly, similar to what many weather apps use).  
- **Locations** are resolved for **Rwanda** so district names match **real places**.  
- The **map** uses **OpenStreetMap** — a widely used, community-built world map.  
- **Registrations** on **My alerts** are stored **in the browser on that device** so users can see their own list; explain that **clearing browser data** removes that list — good for transparency.

*If the audience asks “is it official government data?” — answer honestly: it is **decision-support** from public forecast and map services, not a replacement for national meteorological bulletins unless you partner with an agency.*

---

## 7. Design and visuals (for a “look and feel” slide)

- **Brand colours** are consistent across all pages (**greens, teals, cream background**).  
- **Photos** come from your **`images/`** folder (`crop.jpeg`, `cropb.jpeg`, …) for a **cohesive, local** look.  
- Layout is **responsive**: usable on **phone** and **desktop**.  
- **Navigation** is the same on every page: **Home, Register, My alerts, About, Contact**.

---

## 8. Who benefits (stakeholders slide)

- **Smallholder farmers** — quicker read on **weather risk** and **registration** in one flow.  
- **Cooperatives and extension** — can **demonstrate** a simple tool for **awareness** and **data capture**.  
- **Training and education** — suitable as a **student or programme** showcase (**e.g. programme branding** you use on the site).

---

## 9. Limitations to mention honestly (builds credibility)

- **Contact** uses the visitor’s **email app**; delivery depends on their **mail provider**.  
- **My alerts** is a **local list** on that **browser** — not a central government database unless you **connect** a backend later.  
- **Forecast accuracy** varies by **location and time**; position AgriAlert as **planning support**, not the only source in **severe weather**.

## Quick copy-paste: speaker notes (2-minute version)

AgriAlert is a website called Smart Farmer Weather Alert. On the home page you choose a district in Rwanda and see current weather and a five-day forecast, plus a small map. Farmers can register their district, crop, and how they want to be reached. The My alerts page shows what they saved. About explains the purpose, and Contact lets them send an enquiry by email. The design uses clear colours and local photos. The data comes from public weather and mapping services, so it supports day-to-day decisions while we stay honest about what is local versus what could be added later, like central storage or SMS.
